The game we are going to model is the card game Blackjack. The player’s goal is to reach 21 or get as close to 21 in card amount as possible, without going over. What makes this game interesting is that the player does not know what card they are going to receive next, so they will have to use their best judgment to see if they can beat the “dealer” (which will be a computer player) to 21. It is also a game of chance, so the player will have to make a quick decision based on their cards as well as what they think the dealer has. The main appeal of the game is a chance at winning money, because it is a game designed for gambling. 
Along with the standard options of “hitting” which adds a card to the player’s hand, and “standing” which ends a player’s hand, you can also “double down”, “split”, “insure”, and bet for “Perfect Pairs” on your hand.  “Splitting” splits a hand of two cards into 2 hands of one card. This forces you to draw once on both hands and from there you can perform any typical action. “Double down” in blackjack means to double your initial bet after receiving your first two cards. The core gameplay loop will consist of  a player starting with an initial amount of money/chip value, and getting dealt a starting hand of cards. The dealer will also be dealt this initial hand, and from there the game will commence. The player and dealer will get a singular card to add to their amount if they decide they want to “hit”, and the dealer will do the same. This will keep going until the player decides they want to “stand”, they bust, or if the dealer wins the round. This will then repeat over and over until the player ends the game.
